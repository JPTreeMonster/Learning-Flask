About me

I seek challenge, adventure and growth. I have cycled from Bilbao to Venice, learnt the python coding language and trained in Mixed Martial Arts. These challenges developed my skills of focus, resilience and an eagerness to learn.

    Artwork

    I have painted for many years, including for a personal project called Tree Monster Art. I like using acrylics and drawing to create high contrast, visually striking artwork. My art usually has a theme or idea it depicts, to tell a story or evoke an idea or emotion.

    Flute

    I started learning to play the flute in Secondary school around the age of 12 or 13. I've kept it up as a past time, however I'm not as good as I used to be. Although I only have a Grade 1 Distinction ABRSM ceritificate in flute. However, at my best, during which I didn't want to do further exams in flute, my teacher said I could play upto Grade 7 music.

    Gardener

    I've always enjoyed nature and being outside. This was in part inspired by my Grandad who I helped in his garden. Later on this lead me to horticulture and my degree in Landscape Architecture. I have helped build Silver, Gold and Platinum award winning show gardens, worked on privagte estate's and amassed lots of experience.

    Mixed Martial Arts

    I started Mixed Martial Arts, known as MMA, during Univeristy when I did kickboxing. This later lead to me doing some Taekwondo and eventually MMA. I trained at an MMA gym in Manchester alongside cage fighters and belt winners at Predators MMA. I am currently training with Cardiff University MMA society and plan to continue it in one form or another until I die, hopefully of natural causes.

    Landscape Architecture

    I did an Undergraduate degree in Landscape Architecture, or LA, at the University of Gloucestershire in Cheltenham before working as a Graduate Landscape Architect in Manchester. However I eventually quit after a life quake, causing a cycle journey through Europe and am my career pivot into tech.

    Rugby

    Partially during my Undergraduate days and then more when I was back home I played Rugby as a winger for a while. I really enjoyed it, learned loads and made some great friends too. I currently don't have time for it in my life but it is something I do immensely enjoy.
